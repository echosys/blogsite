**1. No use of cross**

* **JW Stance:** They believe Jesus died on a torture stake, not a cross, and that the cross is a pagan symbol.
* **Biblical Counter-perspective (Broader Christian View):**

  * The New Testament frequently speaks of the "cross" as the central symbol of Christ's sacrifice and atonement. While the exact shape of the instrument of execution is debated by some, the *meaning* of the cross as the place of salvation is paramount.
  * **Galatians 6:14:** "But far be it from me to boast except in the cross of our Lord Jesus Christ, by which the world has been crucified to me, and I to the world." (This verse emphasizes the *glory* and central importance of the cross for salvation, not its physical form or historical pagan associations.)
  * **1 Corinthians 1:18:** "For the word of the cross is folly to those who are perishing, but to us who are being saved it is the power of God." (Again, highlighting the salvific power of the cross.)
  * The focus for most Christians is on the *event* and *meaning* of Christ's sacrifice on the cross, not on the material object itself as an idol.

**2. No birthdays, holidays (Spring Festival, Lantern, Dragon Boat Festival, Qingming, Easter)**

* **JW Stance:** They often view these as having pagan origins, or as self-exalting (birthdays), or as not explicitly commanded in the Bible.
* **Biblical Counter-perspective (Broader Christian View):**

  * The New Testament gives believers freedom in matters of conscience regarding days and festivals, as long as it doesn't lead to sin or compromise core beliefs.
  * **Romans 14:5-6:** "One person esteems one day as better than another, while another esteems all days alike. Each one should be fully convinced in his own mind. The one who observes the day, observes it in honor of the Lord. The one who eats, eats in honor of the Lord, since he gives thanks to God, while the one who abstains, abstains in honor of the Lord and gives thanks to God." (This strongly suggests that Christians have liberty in observing or not observing specific days, and that it's a matter of conscience before God, not a strict prohibition.)
  * **Colossians 2:16-17:** "Therefore let no one pass judgment on you in questions of food and drink, or with regard to a festival or a new moon or a Sabbath. These are a shadow of the things to come, but the substance belongs to Christ." (This implies that strict adherence to specific ceremonial days is not what defines true faith in Christ.)
  * While holidays might have historical roots, for Christians, celebrations like Easter are specifically about commemorating central biblical events (resurrection of Christ), and birthdays can be seen as celebrating God's gift of life.

**3. No meat with blood / No transfusion**

* **JW Stance:** They interpret Old Testament commands against consuming blood (Leviticus 17:10-14, Deuteronomy 12:23) and the New Testament directive to "abstain from blood" (Acts 15:29) as prohibiting blood transfusions, because "blood is life" and sacred to God.
* **Biblical Counter-perspective (Broader Christian View):**

  * Many theologians argue that the Old Testament prohibitions on consuming blood were primarily dietary laws and connected to the sacrificial system, emphasizing the sacredness of life and atonement.
  * **Acts 15:29:** The instruction to "abstain from blood" in the New Testament (Jerusalem Council) was primarily about maintaining unity between Jewish and Gentile converts and avoiding practices associated with idolatry or pagan rituals (where blood was consumed in rites). It is seen by many as a dietary and cultural concession, not a medical prohibition.
  * **Luke 10:25-37 (Parable of the Good Samaritan):** This parable emphasizes loving your neighbor and actively helping those in need, even at personal cost. Many Christians argue that saving a life through medical intervention, including blood transfusions, aligns with this principle of loving compassion and the sanctity of life.
  * **Matthew 12:10-12 (Jesus healing on the Sabbath):** Jesus prioritized saving life over rigid adherence to law. "Is it lawful to heal on the Sabbath, or to kill?" He asks. This illustrates a principle that life-saving measures can take precedence over strict interpretations of rules. A blood transfusion is a life-saving medical procedure, not an act of "eating" blood.
